Mashed Potatos

If you love good, old-fashioned mashed potatoes, this is the perfect recipe. Made with Idaho potatoes, milk, butter, and optional garlic, this go-to recipe for simple, homemade mashed potatoes is a classic holiday dish. Learn how to make mashed potatoes that come out smooth, creamy, and delicious every time. Serve with gravy or extra butter on top.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs of Potatos, peeled and quartered
  • 3 cloves of Garlic, peeled *Optional*
  • 1 cup of Milk
  • 2 Tbsp of Butter
  • Salt and Pepper to taste

Cooking Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Add potatos and garlic, lower heat to medium and allow to cook for 20 minutes.
  2. When potatos are slightly less cooked than fork tender, heat the butter and milk together until butter is melted.
  3. Drain water from potatos, and add potatos back into the dry pot with heat on low to evaporate remaining water.
  4. Slowly add milk and butter mixture, be sure to take care when mixing. Over mixing the potatos will cause a gummy texture to develop in the potatos, leading to a less than ideal dish.
  5. Finish with Salt, Pepper, and any other fixings you see fit.
  6. Enjoy!
